# De-Slop — Kill the AI Writing Tics

A lightweight text-cleanup skill that strips common AI writing tells from copy and makes it sound like a human wrote it. Designed for the Armchair Futurist's voice: warm, direct, existential — not over-produced.

## What It Fixes

### 1. Em-dash Overuse (the #1 tell)
AI loves dramatic em-dashes (—). Humans use them sparingly.

**Rule:** Max one em-dash per paragraph. Replace extras:
- Use `:` before lists or explanations — cleaner than an em-dash
- Use `;` for closely related clauses — softer break than an em-dash
- Use a regular `,` or just start a new sentence

**Bad:** "The edge of change is lonely — you need a guide — not another tool — and that's what I offer."
**Good:** "The edge of change is lonely. You need a guide, not another tool. That's what I offer."

### 2. Regular Dash for Compound Words
Use a regular hyphen (`-`) for compound modifiers, not an em-dash:
- "human-first execution"
- "one-on-one coaching"
- "psychology-led adoption"

### 3. The AI Hall of Shame — Words to Kill
Strip these on sight:

| Kill | Replace With |
|---|---|
| delve / delve into | explore, look at, talk about |
| landscape (as metaphor) | field, space, world — or just delete |
| tapestry | nothing. Just say what you mean |
| testament (to) | sign of, proof of, shows |
| navigate (overused) | handle, move through, deal with |
| it's worth noting that | delete entirely. Just say the thing |
| in today's rapidly evolving | delete. Say "now" or nothing |
| let's dive in | just start talking |
| not only... but also | "and" or a new sentence |
| essentially / fundamentally / ultimately | delete 80% of these |
| the reality is / the truth is | just state the thing directly |
| it goes without saying | then don't say it |
| a myriad of | many, lots of, countless |
| serves as | is, acts as |
| plays a crucial role in | matters, is key, helps |
| first and foremost | first — or just start |
| in order to | to |
| due to the fact that | because |
| at the end of the day | delete |
| journey (as business metaphor) | work, process, experience |
| leverage (as verb, overused) | use, build on, apply |

### 4. Over-formal Transitions
| Kill | Replace With |
|---|---|
| Moreover, | And, Also, — or nothing |
| Furthermore, | Also, Plus, — or nothing |
| Nevertheless, | But, Still, Even so |
| Consequently, | So, That's why |
| In addition, | Also, And |
| Subsequently, | Then, After that, Next |

### 5. Needless Adverbs
| Kill | Keep (rarely) |
|---|---|
| literally | almost never |
| basically | almost never |
| actually | when it signals a genuine contradiction |
| simply | when it's actually simple |
| essentially | never |
| incredibly | never in business writing |
| truly | once per page max |
| very | almost never |
| really | almost never |

### 6. Sentence Rhythm
AI writes in even, predictable sentences. Humans vary length.

**Fix:**
- After a long sentence (25+ words), follow it with a short one (5-10 words). Like this.
- Start a sentence with "And" or "But" sometimes. Real writers do.
- Use sentence fragments occasionally. For emphasis. When it matters.
- Vary paragraph length. Keep them guessing.

### 7. Lists That Sound Like Robots
AI writes "First... Second... Third..." Humans don't.

**Fix:**
- Don't enumerate unless it's actually instructional
- Use bullet lists sparingly
- When listing in prose, use "and" or commas

## When to Use

- After generating any copy with an AI
- Before publishing blog posts, landing page copy, or newsletters
- When editing anything that needs to sound human

## When NOT to Use

- Technical documentation where precision matters more than voice
- Code comments
- Legal copy

## Before/After Examples

### Before (AI-generated)
"The landscape of AI adoption is rapidly evolving — it's worth noting that many organizations are struggling to navigate this complex terrain. First and foremost, the accountability gap serves as a crucial barrier to successful implementation."

### After (de-slopped)
"AI adoption is moving fast. Most organizations are struggling — not with the technology, but with the gap between what AI outputs and what actually gets done."

### Before (AI-generated)
"It's worth noting that the journey toward AI maturity is not a linear one. Organizations must navigate a complex landscape of tools, processes, and cultural shifts."

### After (de-slopped)
"AI maturity isn't a straight line. You're dealing with tools, processes, and culture all at once — and they don't move at the same speed."
